Earth Information
Center

For more than 50 years, NASA satellites have provided data on Earth's land
, water, air, temperature, and climate. NASA's Earth Information Center allows
visitors to see how our planet is changing in six key areas: sea level rise and
coastal impacts, health and air quality, wildfires, greenhouse
gases, sustainable energy, and agriculture.

Image Of The Day

Astronaut Suni Williams and
Astrobee

NASA astronaut and Expedition 72 Commander Suni Williams checks
out the Astrobee robotic free-flyer in the Kibo laboratory module
outfitted with tentacle-like arms containing gecko-like
adhesive pads to demonstrate satellite capture techniques. Development
of this robotic technology may increase the life span
of satellites and enable the removal of space debris.
